Help & FAQ

Last Updated: July 11, 2026

Quick answers to common questions about Nepsis. For anything not covered here, see Contact Support.

How do I grant Screen Time permission?

Nepsis needs Apple's Screen Time authorization to block apps. When prompted, tap Continue and approve access in the system dialog. If you previously denied it, go to Settings → Screen Time and ensure the app has permission, or reinstall Nepsis and accept when asked.

You can also check status in Nepsis under Settings → Permissions & Diagnostics.

Why isn't my block working?

Common causes:

  • Screen Time permission was not granted or was revoked in iOS Settings
  • The rule schedule hasn't started yet, or the rule is paused
  • The app you want to block wasn't selected in the rule (Screen Time requires you to pick apps via Apple's picker)
  • iOS Focus modes or Downtime settings may interact with restrictions — check system Screen Time settings

Try toggling the rule off and on, and verify permissions in Permissions & Diagnostics.

What is Strict mode?

Strict mode adds extra friction before you can disable a block or change a rule — for example, requiring a passcode or a waiting period. It helps when you want stronger commitment to your limits.

If you set a passcode, a secure hash of it is stored locally in the iOS Keychain — not the passcode itself — and is not sent to us. You can optionally use Face ID or Touch ID to unlock Strict gates on the same device.

I forgot my Strict mode passcode. What can I do?

Only a hash of your passcode is stored on your device; we cannot recover or reset it remotely. If you are locked out, you may need to remove and reinstall the app (which clears local data) or reset related Keychain entries — note this will remove your local rules and settings.

How does iCloud sync work?

When your device is signed into iCloud, Nepsis automatically syncs rule metadata (names, schedules, protection/Strict preferences, and Look/settings metadata) between your devices on the same iCloud account via Apple's iCloud Key-Value Storage. There is currently no separate sync on/off switch inside Nepsis settings.

Apple limitation: Screen Time app and category selections (tokens) cannot be synced between devices. After syncing, you may need to re-select which apps to block on each iPhone or iPad. Your passcode hash also stays local to each device. This is an iOS platform restriction, not a Nepsis bug.

How do I restore my subscription?

Open Nepsis, go to the subscription screen or Settings, and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you are signed into the same Apple ID used for the original purchase.

If restore doesn't work, confirm the subscription is active under Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions on your device.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed by Apple, not inside Nepsis:

  1. Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions
  2. Select Nepsis → Cancel Subscription

See our Terms of Use for billing details.

What data does Nepsis collect?

Nepsis is local-first: your Screen Time usage and blocks stay on your device. Rule metadata may sync via Apple's iCloud when available on the device. Subscriptions use RevenueCat with an anonymous User ID — no account sign-up required. Optional Camera, Microphone, Speech Recognition, and Face ID features process data on-device only.

Full details: Privacy Policy.

Do I need an account?

No. Nepsis does not require registration. Your Apple ID is used by Apple for App Store purchases, and iCloud (when signed in on the device) may sync rule metadata automatically — there is no separate Nepsis account or in-app sync toggle.

Notifications aren't working

Enable notifications for Nepsis in Settings → Notifications → Nepsis. Check Permissions & Diagnostics in the app for current status.
